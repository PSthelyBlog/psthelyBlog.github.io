If you've ever used a chatbot that felt like talking to a vending machine — press a button, get a pre-packaged answer — Claude is designed to be something different. Claude is an AI assistant built by Anthropic to act as a thinking partner: one that can reason through problems with you, adapt to your needs, and handle a wide range of professional tasks. Whether you're drafting a strategy document, debugging a formula, learning a new concept, or analyzing hundreds of pages of research, Claude is built to work alongside you — not just respond to you.

This post is the starting point for everything else in the course. Before you learn how to prompt Claude, organize your work in Projects, or connect it to the tools you already use, it helps to understand what Claude actually is, how it works at a high level, and what makes it different from simpler AI tools you may have encountered before.


## How Claude Works: A Brief Look Under the Hood

At its core, Claude is what's known as a **large language model**, or LLM. If that term is new to you, here's the short version: an LLM is a type of AI that has been trained on vast amounts of text — books, articles, code, conversations — and has learned to recognize patterns in language. When you type a message to Claude, it draws on those patterns to generate a response that is relevant, coherent, and (ideally) useful.

But Claude is not just a pattern-matching machine. What separates it from earlier chatbots — the kind that could answer a narrow set of scripted questions — is the depth and flexibility of its understanding. Claude can follow multi-step reasoning, hold context across a long conversation, adapt its tone and style to your preferences, and work across a genuinely broad range of tasks. It doesn't have a fixed menu of things it can do. You shape the conversation, and Claude meets you where you are.

That said, Claude has limits. It can produce responses that sound confident but are factually wrong — a phenomenon sometimes called "hallucination." It doesn't have access to the internet by default (though web search can be enabled), and its training data has a cutoff date, meaning it may not know about very recent events unless it searches for them. Understanding these boundaries is part of using Claude effectively, and we'll cover strategies for working within them later in the course.


## The Principles Behind Claude: Helpful, Harmless, and Honest

What makes Claude distinct isn't just the technology underneath — it's the principles guiding how it behaves. Anthropic developed Claude using an approach called **Constitutional AI**, which trains the model to be helpful, harmless, and honest. These aren't just marketing words; they reflect deliberate design choices that affect how Claude responds to you every day.

**Helpful** means Claude is designed to give you substantive, useful answers. It doesn't dodge questions unnecessarily or give you vague non-answers when a clear response is possible. If you ask for help writing a project brief, Claude will actually write one — not just tell you what a project brief should contain.

**Harmless** means Claude is trained to avoid producing content that could cause harm. It won't help you do things that are dangerous or unethical, and it's designed to handle sensitive topics with care. This doesn't mean Claude is overly cautious or refuses to engage with complex subjects — it means it tries to be responsible in how it does so.

**Honest** means Claude is transparent about what it knows and doesn't know. When it's uncertain, it's designed to say so rather than fabricate an answer. This is an important distinction from tools that always project confidence regardless of accuracy. Claude won't always get things right, but it's built to be forthcoming about its limitations.

These three principles work together to create an assistant you can trust as a professional collaborator — not blindly, but with a reasonable degree of confidence that it's trying to give you good information in good faith.


## What Can Claude Actually Do?

Claude's capabilities span a broad set of professional tasks. According to the official documentation, you can use Claude to summarize lengthy documents, draft and refine written content, answer questions about complex topics, write and debug code, conduct research and analysis, translate between languages, brainstorm ideas, and work through multi-step problems. Think of it less as a tool that does one thing well and more as a versatile collaborator you can point at many different kinds of work.

Here's a closer look at some of the most common use cases:

**Summarization.** Claude excels at distilling long content into concise summaries. Whether it's a 50-page report, a dense legal document, or a long email thread, you can paste or upload the content and ask Claude to pull out the key points. This alone can save hours of reading time each week.

**Writing and editing.** From first drafts to final polish, Claude can help at every stage of the writing process. You might ask it to draft a blog post, rewrite a paragraph in a more conversational tone, or proofread a document for errors. Claude adapts to the style and voice you specify — formal for a client proposal, casual for an internal Slack message, technical for a developer audience.

**Research and analysis.** Claude can help you explore topics, compare options, and synthesize information from multiple sources. When web search is enabled, it can pull in current information from the internet. When used within a Project (more on this in a later lesson), it can draw on documents you've uploaded as a knowledge base.

**Coding.** Claude is a capable programming assistant. It can write code, explain what existing code does, debug errors, and suggest improvements. It works across all major programming languages, with particular strength in Python, JavaScript, and others commonly used in professional settings.

**Learning and explanation.** One of Claude's most underappreciated strengths is its ability to teach. You can ask it to explain a concept you're unfamiliar with — from financial modeling to molecular biology to how a specific software tool works — and it will break it down in clear, accessible language. You can follow up with questions, ask for examples, or request a different angle until the concept clicks.

**Brainstorming and ideation.** Claude makes a strong sounding board. You can use it to generate ideas, explore different perspectives on a problem, or pressure-test a strategy. It's particularly useful when you're stuck or want to think through something from an angle you hadn't considered.

**Translation.** Claude is extensively trained in English and performs well in many other languages, with particular strength in Portuguese, French, and German. You can ask it to translate content between languages or to write original content in a language other than English.

This is not an exhaustive list — it's a starting point. The more you use Claude, the more you'll discover tasks where it can save you time or improve the quality of your work.


## What Makes Claude Different

Several design choices make Claude stand out as a professional thinking partner rather than a simple question-answering tool.

### Steerability

Claude is designed to take direction. You can tell it how you want it to behave — its tone, its level of detail, its formatting, its persona — and it will follow your lead with less friction than many other AI tools. This is what Anthropic calls "steerability," and it matters more than you might think. It means Claude doesn't impose a one-size-fits-all style on every interaction. If you want bullet points, you get bullet points. If you want a conversational paragraph, you get that instead. If you want Claude to adopt the voice of a senior analyst writing for a board of directors, you can ask for that too.

This steerability extends beyond individual conversations. Claude offers customizable communication styles — both preset options and custom styles you can create from your own writing samples — so it can match your preferred voice consistently across every interaction.

### A Large Context Window

One of Claude's most practically significant features is its **context window** — the amount of information it can hold in its working memory during a single conversation. Claude's context window is 200,000 tokens, which translates to roughly 500 pages of text. Enterprise plans expand this even further, to 500,000 tokens on certain models.

What does this mean in practice? It means you can upload a long document — an entire contract, a quarterly earnings report, a research paper — and ask Claude to analyze it, summarize it, or answer questions about it without losing track of the content. Many AI tools struggle with documents longer than a few pages. Claude can work with entire books.

This large context window also means conversations can go deeper. You can have extended back-and-forth discussions where Claude remembers what you said 50 messages ago and builds on it, rather than losing the thread halfway through.

### Extended Thinking

For particularly complex problems — the kind that require careful, multi-step reasoning — Claude offers a feature called **extended thinking**. When enabled, extended thinking allows Claude to spend more time breaking down a problem, planning its approach, and exploring different angles before delivering a response.

Extended thinking is especially valuable for tasks like solving mathematical problems, debugging intricate code, analyzing philosophical or ethical questions from multiple perspectives, and tackling any problem where a quick answer is likely to miss important nuance. You can even expand Claude's thinking process to see how it arrived at its conclusion, which can be valuable for learning and verification.

Think of it as the difference between asking someone a question in passing and asking them to sit down, think carefully, and give you their best considered answer. Both are useful — extended thinking is for when the stakes or complexity warrant the extra effort.


## Where You Can Access Claude

Claude is available across multiple platforms, designed to meet you wherever you're already working.

**Claude.ai** is the primary web interface — the chat experience most people start with. It's accessible from any modern web browser and is where you'll find the full range of Claude's features, including Projects, Artifacts, Research mode, and connectors to external tools.

**Claude Desktop** is a standalone application for macOS and Windows that adds capabilities beyond the web experience. It includes three distinct modes — Chat, Cowork, and Code — which we'll explore in detail in a later lesson. Desktop-specific features include quick keyboard shortcuts to summon Claude as an overlay, the ability to share your screen or paste screenshots for visual context, voice dictation, and direct access to local files and tools through desktop extensions.

**Claude Mobile** is available on both iOS and Android, giving you access to your conversations, Projects, and preferences on the go. Everything syncs across devices, so a conversation started on your laptop continues seamlessly on your phone.

Beyond these core interfaces, Claude is also available in specialized forms tailored to specific work contexts: **Claude Code** for developers working in the terminal or IDE, **Claude in Slack** for team communication, **Claude for Excel** for spreadsheet work, and **Claude in Chrome** for browser-based tasks. Each of these products brings Claude's capabilities directly into the tools you already use, rather than asking you to switch contexts. We'll cover each of these in depth later in the course.


## Choosing a Plan

Claude is available across several plan tiers, each designed for different levels of use.

The **Free** plan gives you access to Claude with basic usage limits — a good way to explore what Claude can do before committing to a subscription. You can start conversations, upload files, and experience Claude's core capabilities.

The **Pro** plan is designed for individual professionals who want more capacity, priority access during peak times, early access to new features, the ability to switch between different Claude models, and access to Projects and knowledge bases.

The **Max** plan comes in two tiers (5x and 20x) offering substantially more usage than Pro, designed for power users who work with Claude throughout the day. Max plans also include access to Claude's most advanced features and products.

The **Team** plan is built for organizations of up to 150 people, with collaborative workspaces, shared Projects, and administrative controls. Teams can mix and match Standard and Premium seats depending on each member's usage needs.

The **Enterprise** plan is for larger organizations that need advanced security features, compliance tools, enhanced context windows, and dedicated support. Enterprise plans include audit logs, SCIM provisioning, a compliance API, and the ability to connect Claude to organizational tools like Google Drive, Gmail, GitHub, and Slack.

Whichever plan you choose, your conversations and preferences sync across all devices and platforms — web, desktop, and mobile. You can start a conversation on your laptop and pick it up on your phone without missing a beat.


## What Claude Is Not

Understanding what Claude is also means understanding what it isn't. A few clarifications are worth making upfront.

**Claude is not a search engine.** While Claude can search the web when that feature is enabled, its primary mode of operation is drawing on its training knowledge and the context you provide. It reasons, synthesizes, and generates — it doesn't just retrieve links.

**Claude is not infallible.** Like all large language models, Claude can make mistakes. It can present incorrect information confidently, misunderstand your intent, or produce responses that miss the mark. The course will teach you how to verify Claude's outputs, give effective feedback, and iterate toward better results. The right mental model is to think of Claude as a highly capable but imperfect collaborator — one whose work you should review, especially for high-stakes tasks.

**Claude is not a replacement for human judgment.** Claude is a tool that amplifies your capabilities. It can draft, research, analyze, and suggest — but the final decisions, especially in areas like legal, financial, and medical domains, should always involve human expertise and verification.
