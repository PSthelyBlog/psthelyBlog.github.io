# What Is Claude? A Professional's Introduction to AI That Works With You

If you've encountered AI chatbots before and walked away a little underwhelmed — answers that felt canned, an assistant that couldn't track a multi-step conversation, a tool you used once and forgot about — then understanding what Claude actually is requires setting aside that prior experience and starting fresh.

Claude is not a chatbot in the classic sense. It's an AI assistant built by Anthropic and designed to function as a genuine thinking partner: something you can reason through problems with, ask to produce real work, push back on, and direct across a wide range of professional tasks. The difference isn't just marketing language. It reflects meaningful choices in how the system was designed and what it was built to do.

This post covers what Claude is, what guides how it behaves, what it can do, and how you can access it — giving you the foundation you need before diving into any of the more specific features covered later in this course.

---

## The Technology Underneath: What Is a Large Language Model?

Claude is built on a type of technology called a **large language model**, or LLM. That phrase gets used a lot without explanation, so it's worth a brief, plain-language description.

A large language model is a system trained on enormous amounts of written text — books, articles, websites, code, and more — to learn patterns in how language works. During training, the model develops an ability to generate coherent, contextually relevant text in response to a prompt. It's not looking up answers in a database, and it's not running a search engine. It's generating responses by drawing on a vast, internalized understanding of language and the topics that language has been used to describe.

This is why Claude can write a legal memo, explain a chemistry concept, debug your code, translate a paragraph into French, and brainstorm marketing slogans — often in the same conversation. The underlying capability is flexible and generalist, not narrowly specialized.

## What Makes Claude Different: Constitutional AI

The technology alone doesn't explain why Claude behaves the way it does. That comes down to how Anthropic trained it.

Anthropic developed Claude using an approach called **Constitutional AI**. The idea, in plain terms, is this: rather than only training an AI to be useful, you also train it to follow a set of principles that guide its behavior toward being helpful, harmless, and honest.

In practice, this shapes the experience of working with Claude in a few important ways:

- **It's transparent about what it doesn't know.** Claude won't confidently fabricate an answer when it's uncertain. It will flag its own limitations, acknowledge when something might be wrong, and encourage you to verify important information.
- **It can be steered.** If you tell Claude to change its tone, approach a problem differently, or focus only on a specific aspect of a topic, it responds to that direction — reliably and without requiring you to fight against default behaviors.
- **It tries to be genuinely useful, not just pleasant.** Claude is designed to give you substantive help, not empty agreement. It will push back on faulty assumptions and offer alternative perspectives when that's more useful than nodding along.

This doesn't mean Claude is perfect, or that it never makes mistakes — it does. But the principles guiding its design mean those mistakes tend to look different from a system optimized only for engagement or fluency.

## What Claude Can Actually Do

Claude's capabilities span a wide range of professional tasks. Here's an honest overview of where it genuinely performs well:

### Writing and Communication
Claude can help you draft, refine, and adapt written content across almost any format: emails, reports, presentations, proposals, social media posts, meeting notes, blog posts, even long-form documents. It handles tone adjustments, structural revisions, and rewriting for different audiences. If you have a house style or a specific voice you want to maintain, you can describe it and Claude will adapt.

### Summarization
One of Claude's strongest practical uses is condensing long material. Upload a lengthy document, a stack of articles, or a dense report, and ask Claude to pull out the key points, produce an executive summary, or identify the most relevant sections for your specific question. This saves meaningful time on tasks that would otherwise require careful reading of material you need a quick handle on.

### Research and Analysis
Claude can help you structure a research question, synthesize information across multiple sources, identify gaps in an argument, analyze trends in data, or think through the implications of a finding. It works best as a thinking partner here — something that helps you go faster and think more rigorously — rather than as a primary source. (For tasks requiring current information from the web, Claude has separate research and web search modes covered later in this course.)

### Question and Answer
You can ask Claude to explain complex concepts, teach you how something works, or answer questions about topics you're encountering for the first time. Whether it's a regulatory framework, a technical system, a historical event, or a business concept, Claude can explain it in plain language and adjust the depth based on how much detail you want.

### Coding
Claude is a capable coding assistant. It can write code from scratch based on a plain-English description, debug errors, explain what an unfamiliar piece of code does, suggest improvements, and help you work in languages you're less familiar with. It's strongest in Python and other widely used languages, but it has meaningful capability across most major programming languages.

### Brainstorming and Problem-Solving
Because Claude can hold a long, substantive conversation, it works well as a thought partner when you're working through an ambiguous problem. You can use it to stress-test ideas, generate options you hadn't considered, play devil's advocate, or structure a messy situation into something cleaner and more actionable.

### Translation
Claude can translate across languages and, within a conversation, adapt content for different regional or cultural contexts. Its strongest translation performance is in widely spoken languages; less common languages may see more variable results.

## A Distinctive Feature: The Context Window

One of the most practically important aspects of Claude is something called the **context window** — the amount of text Claude can hold in its "working memory" during a single conversation.

Claude supports a context window of up to 200,000 tokens. Tokens are units of text roughly equivalent to pieces of words, so 200,000 tokens corresponds to roughly 500 pages of text. This is unusually large.

What does that mean in practice? It means you can paste in an entire contract, a full research report, a long email thread, or multiple documents at once — and Claude can work across all of that material in a single conversation. You don't have to summarize things first or manually piece together context. Claude can hold it all and reason across it.

This is what distinguishes Claude from a tool you use for quick, isolated queries. It's capable of sustained, deep work on substantial material.

## Extended Thinking: A Mode for Complex Problems

For tasks that require careful, multi-step reasoning — analyzing competing options, working through a legal or financial question, evaluating a complex strategy — Claude's Opus and Sonnet models offer a mode called **extended thinking**.

When extended thinking is enabled, Claude takes more time before responding to reason through the problem systematically before producing an answer. Think of it as the difference between a quick answer and a deliberate one. For nuanced, high-stakes tasks where getting the reasoning right matters more than getting an answer quickly, extended thinking can produce meaningfully better results.

## How to Access Claude

Claude is available across several platforms, and your conversations and preferences stay synchronized across all of them.

**Claude.ai** is the primary web interface, accessible from any browser at claude.ai. This is where most people start and where the full range of Claude's features is available — including Projects, Artifacts, Research mode, and the ability to connect external tools.

**Mobile apps** are available for both iOS and Android, giving you access to Claude on your phone with the same conversation history as the web interface.

**Claude Desktop** is a native desktop application for macOS and Windows. It includes everything in the web interface plus desktop-specific features: a quick-entry overlay you can summon from any app with a keyboard shortcut, screenshot capture, voice dictation, and access to Cowork — a mode for sustained, complex work that can read from and write to your local files.

**Claude Code** is a specialized tool for developers, available in the terminal, inside IDEs like VS Code and JetBrains, and via a web interface. It's designed for agentic coding tasks: writing features from descriptions, navigating unfamiliar codebases, automating repetitive development work.

**Claude in Slack, Claude for Excel, and Claude for Chrome** are product integrations that bring Claude directly into the tools where specific kinds of work already happen. These are covered in depth later in the course.

## Choosing a Plan

Claude is available across a range of plans designed for different levels of use.

The **Free plan** gives you access to Claude with session-based usage limits that reset periodically. It's a reasonable starting point for getting familiar with the tool.

**Pro** is the core paid individual plan, offering significantly more usage than the free tier and priority access during high-traffic periods, along with early access to new features.

**Max** is designed for individuals who use Claude heavily and need more capacity. It comes in two tiers — 5x and 20x the usage of Pro — and unlocks features like Claude in Chrome.

**Team** is for groups working together, offering collaborative workspaces, shared Projects, and usage limits per member rather than across the team as a whole.

**Enterprise** is for larger organizations that need advanced security controls, expanded context windows, audit logs, compliance tooling, and dedicated support.

All paid plans — from Pro through Enterprise — include access to Claude on web, desktop, and mobile, with conversations and settings syncing across devices.

## Putting It Together: Claude as a Thinking Partner

The most useful way to think about Claude isn't as a search engine, a writing tool, or a question-answering machine — though it can do all of those things. The most useful frame is a **thinking partner**: something that can work through problems with you, handle a wide range of professional tasks, and improve with direction.

That means the way you get value from Claude is different from most tools you've encountered before. You don't just ask it a question and take the answer. You engage with it, iterate on the output, redirect it when it misses the mark, and use it to do work that would otherwise take significantly more of your own time.

The rest of this course is about how to do that well — starting with how to have your first productive conversation.
